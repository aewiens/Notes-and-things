Coupled-Electron Pair Approximations (CEPAs)

The CEPA methods are techniques for computing dynamic correlation energy on top of a Hartree-Fock (HF) reference. For convenience, define a correlation energy Ec and a correlated Hamiltonian Ĥc:

Ec = Ee - EHF Ĥc = Ĥe - EHF
Obeying the convention that indices i,j,k... correspond to occupied orbitals and a,b,c,... to virtual orbitals, we also define a doubles excitation operator:
2|Φ⟩ = ∑ ijabtijab|Φ ijab⟩.
With these definitions and a Hartree-Fock reference |Φ⟩, the CCD equations are:
Ec = ⟨Φ|Ĥc|Φ⟩ (1)
Ectijab = ⟨Φ ijab|Ĥ c|Φ⟩ + ⟨Φijab|Ĥ c|Φ⟩ + ⟨Φijab|Ĥ c2|Φ⟩  (2)
where (1) is called the CCD energy expression and (2) is the CCD amplitude equations for the excitation ij → ab. Both expressions can be simplified using Slater rules or Wick’s theorem.
The simplified energy expression (1) is:

The CCD expression for ΔL is nonlinear in the t-amplitudes (they are multiplied by each other). This is what makes the coupled-cluster equations very complex and difficult to parallelize in a production code. Thus, the goal of the CEPA methods is to include a linear approximation for ΔL.
